Lynn's Story

Early Years

Lynn Elizabeth Miller Nichols was born on June 15, 1955 in South Bend, Indiana, the third child and only daughter of Alexis Thielens Miller and Carolyn Plasman Miller. Lynn is survived by husband Joe, daughter Elizabeth, age 24, son Eric, age 19, and brothers Scott, age____, of Arlington, VA, and Ross, age ___ , of Bernardsville, NJ.

Breaking Out On Her Own

Lynn’s family moved to Fox Point, a suburb of Milwaukee, while she was still an infant. Growing up, Lynn adored the family basset hound, Sam, her rabbits (the “bunnies”), riding horses at summer camp in Wyoming, her bike, Doctor’s Park and the “House on Allen Lane” where she lived. Lynn attended University School Milwaukee where she was a cheerleader and played field hockey. She graduated high school in 1973 and went on to DePauw University in Greencastle, Indiana. She joined Pi Beta Phi social sorority and decided to study nursing, as she was greatly impacted by the untimely death of her father in 1975. Lynn received her BSN in 1977 and worked as a floor nurse in Indianapolis for a few years before feeling the itch to move somewhere new. Lynn loaded up her old, big 1970 Caprice convertible with all of her stuff, plus two big dogs, and took off for Phoenix by herself in 1981.

Professional Life

Lynn was an operating room circulating nurse and quickly came to call Good Samaritan hospital in downtown Phoenix her home. She was known for being the social coordinator for the Good Samaritan operating room staff, and particularly enjoyed biking, playing coed soccer and exploring the Arizona outdoors in her spare time. Havasupai in particular was one of her favorite places. Like everyone else in Arizona in the 80s, she was also a realtor on the side. Her mother and brothers came to visit the sunshine often, and Lynn loved spending time with her niece Stephanie and nephew Alexis.

Falling In Love

Lynn and Joe met in 1989, with masks-on in the Good Samaritan operating room. Joe was a resident from University of Arizona medical school, up from Tucson for the summer. They continued their relationship long distance for a few years, until Joe accepted a job offer and moved up to Phoenix in the fall of 1991. Later that year during the holiday season, Joe proposed and Lynn said yes. They were married at the Arizona Biltmore in May 1992.

Starting A Family

The following spring, the big announcement came. Elizabeth was due in early January ‘94. After the first of many remodels, Joe and Lynn moved into their current home in December ‘93, and Elizabeth was born ahead of schedule a few weeks later on the 26th. Lynn was determined that Elizabeth should have a sibling, and despite some difficulty conceiving, Eric was born in May 1999.

Building A Life Together

Lynn loved to share special parts of her own childhood with her kids, like summer camp, piano lessons, sports, scouting, road trips and “mutskies.” The four dogs we had over the years (Henry, Rocky, Cloe and Brady) were cherished members of the family. She loved playing her Broadway show tunes in the car and frequented Disneyland with the kids during spring breaks. She was always quick to volunteer to help out with or chaperone school activities and faithfully attended all sports practices and games, even though neither kid was interested in playing her beloved soccer.

In Her Memory

In late 2009, Lynn was diagnosed with early-onset Alzheimer’s disease. She faced this knowledge with courage, calm, and dignity. We spent some time in the eye of the storm and made the most of our time as a family. She transitioned into an assisted living facility in fall of 2013 and passed away _______. Lynn will be remembered by her family for her stubborn optimism, generosity, independence, love for her adopted desert home and passion for bringing people together.
